5 things to do right now
Use a password manager
One strong unique password per account. Never reuse passwords — if one site leaks, attackers try it everywhere.
Turn on 2-step verification
Even if someone steals your password, they still can't get in without your phone. Enable it on email, Instagram, TikTok.
Check your privacy settings
Set Instagram, Snapchat, and TikTok to private. Strangers don't need to see your location, school, or daily routine.
Keep your apps updated
Updates fix security holes. An unpatched app is an open door. Turn on automatic updates on your phone.
Be careful on public Wi-Fi
Public networks at shopping centres or cafés can be monitored. Avoid logging into banking or email on them.
Think before you post
Photos can reveal your location, school uniform, or home address. Screenshots last forever — even after you delete.
Red flags — stop and think
"You've won a prize — click here to claim it" — this is almost always a scam.
Someone you've only met online asks for photos, money, or to keep your chat secret.
A message that creates urgency — "act now or your account will be deleted."
A link that looks almost right but is slightly off — paypa1.com, g00gle.com.
Someone asks for your password — no legitimate service ever needs it.
Quick security checklist
Password manager installed
2FA on email and social media
Social accounts set to private
Phone has a PIN or Face ID
Auto-updates turned on
Know who to talk to if something goes wrong
If something feels wrong online, tell a trusted adult or report it.
